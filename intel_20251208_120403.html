250 Environmental Orgs Ask Congress for Moratorium on New Data Centers

REGION: Global | 2025-12-08 12:04
Intelligence Brief: Data Center Moratorium Appeal

Subject: Environmental Organizations' Appeal to Congress for a Moratorium on New Data Centers


Executive Summary:

In alignment with the intensifying "AI and cryptocurrency frenzy," a coalition of 250 environmental organizations has lobbied Congress, demanding a temporary suspension of new data center constructions. The coalition underscores the detrimental impacts that these data centers pose to American life, primarily citing the exacerbation of climate change and its subsequent repercussions.


Key Points:

- Coalition: 250 environmental organizations have unified to address the environmental toll of escalating data center deployments.

- Threat to American lives: The organizations allege that the AI and cryptocurrency boom, fuelled by expansive data centers, jeopardizes human wellbeing via accelerated climate change and its ripple effects.

- Cooling Systems: A significant factor contributing to environmental degradation is the extensive energy consumption of data center cooling systems.

- Eemshaven, Data Center, Cooling Systems: Images from Eemshaven's data center demonstrate the magnitude of cooling systems, a central concern for environmental advocates.


Recommendations:

- Congress must take cognizance of environmental concerns and impose a moratorium on the establishment of new data centers.

- Stringent regulations should be considered for existing data centers, focusing on the efficiency of cooling systems, renewable energy usage, and overall environmental impact.

- Encouraging the transition towards greener and more sustainable data center technologies is crucial for mitigating the environmental footprint of the burgeoning digital economy.
